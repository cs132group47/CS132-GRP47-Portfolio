Hi! We are COVestigators.

And this is our data science project, Twitter Analysis of the Mis/Disinformation surrounding Food Supplements and OTC Drugs as cure for COVID-19, which aims to investigate twitter posts from 2020 to 2023 claiming the effectiveness of food supplements and over-the-counter drugs against COVID-19.

Here's an overview of our project.

Motivation

The spread of misinformation in social media platforms like Facebook and Twitter runs rampant amidst the ongoing pandemic. Medical misinformation, in particular, poses a higher risk to the public's health and often negatively impacts the perception and behavior of people. Coupled with the readily accessible online content, this project aims to shed light on the possible reasons of COVID-19 misinformation.

Problem

Online mis/disinformation or most commonly known as 'fake news', challenges the ability of users in making rational and informed decisions in relation to their health. The need to fact-check medical claims about COVID-19 all the more becomes apparent which propagates faster than verified medical posts based on scientific evidences.

Solution

Our group's objective is to raise awareness among social media users on misinformation and its harmful effects on public safety.

Problem Formulation # 1

Research Question

What are the most commonly recommended over-the-counter drugs as cures for COVID-19 in the Philippines on Twitter?


What are the most commonly recommended food supplements as cures for COVID-19 in the Philippines on Twitter?

Hypothesis

Twitter users in the Philippines will commonly recommend over-the-counter drugs such as Biogesic and Bioflu as cures for COVID-19.


Twitter users in the Philippines will commonly recommend food supplements like Lola Remedios and MX3, as cures for COVID-19.

Null Hypothesis

There will be no significant difference in the types of over-the-counter drugs commonly recommended as cures for COVID-19 by Twitter users in the Philippines.


There will be no significant difference in the types of food supplements commonly recommended as cures for COVID-19 by Twitter users in the Philippines.

Problem Formulation # 2

Research Question

How frequently do Twitter users in the Philippines recommend over-the-counter drugs and food supplements as cures for COVID-19?

Hypothesis

Twitter users in the Philippines will frequently recommend over-the-counter drugs and food supplements as cures for COVID-19, with a higher frequency during times of increasing COVID-19 cases.

Null Hypothesis

There will be no significant difference in the frequency of tweets recommending over-the-counter drugs and food supplements as cures for COVID-19 by Twitter users in the Philippines.


Action Plans

Analyze the types of OTC drugs and food supplements commonly recommended as cures for COVID-19 by Twitter users in the Philippines.

Analyze the frequency and posting time of recommendations after news of increased COVID-19 cases have been reported.

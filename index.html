Here's an overview of our project.

Motivation

The spread of misinformation in social media platforms like Facebook and Twitter runs rampant amidst the ongoing pandemic. Medical misinformation, in particular, poses a higher risk to the public's health and often negatively impacts the perception and behavior of people. Coupled with the readily accessible online content, this project aims to shed light on the possible reasons of COVID-19 misinformation.

Problem

Online mis/disinformation or most commonly known as 'fake news', challenges the ability of users in making rational and informed decisions in relation to their health. The need to fact-check medical claims about COVID-19 all the more becomes apparent which propagates faster than verified medical posts based on scientific evidences.

Solution

Our group's objective is to raise awareness among social media users on misinformation and its harmful effects on public safety.

Problem Formulation

Research Question

What events have triggered the spread of mis/disinformation about food supplements and OTC drugs being the cure for COVID-19?

Hypothesis

The timing of the spikes in COVID-19 cases correlates to the spread of mis/disinformation about food supplements and OTC drugs being able to cure COVID-19.

Null Hypothesis

There is no significant correlation between the rise in COVID-19 cases and the spread of mis/disinformation about food supplements and OTC drugs as COVID-19 cure.


Action Plan

Use Event Detection Model and Time Series Analysis to analyze the correlation of certain peak events.

Let's talk about our data exploration.

After obtaining sufficient number of tweets, we now need to explore our data so that we can understand our datasets better and make data analysis easier. We do this by executing the following three data exploration steps: preprocessing, visualization, and feature analysis.

PREPROCESSING

Here are the preprocessing techniques that we utilized to ensure that our data are clean, consistent, and transformed into a desirable format for effective analysis and processing later on.

STEP 1: Check the data structure

The dataset has 151 samples with 152 rows and 19 columns but some columns have missing values. Note that the `Tweet Translated` column contains missing values as this will be later be used to translate tweet text using NLP (natural language processing). Furthermore, we have already dropped certain columns that are not necessary for our data exploration.

STEP 2: Checking for missing values

Our group decided to fill the missing samples with a constant string (i.e. N/A and Philippines) or value (i.e. 0 and 1) instead of dropping them since this will affect the sample size of our project. The following columns contains null values: `Account name`, `Account bio`, `Location`, `Retweets`, and `Tweet Translated`


STEP 3: Handling outliers

In order to determine the columns with outliers, we first need to visualize possible outliers by utilizing distribution graphs and box plots. We will also be using its Interquantile Range (IQR) to calculate the upper and lower limit of our dataframes. After using box plot graphs on the following dataframe, it can be observed that the ff. columns have outliers: `Following`, `Followers`, `Likes`, `Replies`, and `Retweets`. The group decided to use Capping to avoid the loss of information and using a smaller sample size.

STEP 4: Ensuring formatting consistency

In order to produce a cleaner and more organized dataframe, we need to ensure that the format of each column is consistent. This also includes checking the data type and correcting any format-related mistakes. The group decided to a.) manually remove any whitespaces on the raw data source by using this convenient feature called `trim whitespace` in google sheets and b.) use `find and replace` to change the date format. .


STEP 5: Normalization/Standardization/Scaling

Data Normalization refers to the process of aligning numerical columns into its standard scale and distribution of values. We used the min-max feature scaling method to normalize the numerical data for columns Followers, Following, Likes, Replies, and Retweets.

STEP 6: Categorical data encoding

In this step, our group decided to use one hot encoding method to transform categorical variables into numerical values so as to lessen sample bias present in integer encoding. It categorizes two columns, `Account type` and `Content type`, into its binary representation unique to each of its own type.



Natural Language Processing

This part basically cleans unnecessary elements which make our data unorganized. It involves removing entities such as `punctuations`, `special characters`, `emoji/emoticons`, `URL's`, `Hashtags`, `Mentions`, `Alt-text-image descriptions`, `newlines`, and `whitespaces` in our twitter texts column.

Translation

We will be using Natural Language Tookit (NLTK), which is a popular open-source Python library for natural language processing (NLP), to facilitate the translation of tweets from Filipino to English language. As for the reason why English is more convenient in using NLP techniques (i.e. tokenization, stop words removal, stemming, and lemmatization), this is due to having no accurate NLP for the Filipino language yet.

Tokenization and Stop Words Removal

After ensuring that data is properly cleaned and translated to english, we will now employ tokenization and stop word removal. Tokenization refers to the process of separating pieces of texts into 'tokens' in order to break it into easily understandable parts. Stop words removal, on the other hand, removes certain english words that occur frequently in texts such as 'I', 'and', 'the', etc.


Stemming and Lemmatization

Further simplification of multiple words into common root words can make our analysis much easier and improve our model performance later. To achive this, we will make use of stemming and lemmatization. Stemming cuts off the last few characters of a word in an attempt to convert the word into its base form, but it is prone to incorrect meanings and spelling. On the other hand, lemmatization considers the context in its conversion, making it much more accurate.

Let us expound on our data modeling and analysis.

Statistical Testing is used to determine whether to accept or reject the hypothesis of a study. In this case, the group decided to use `Pearson's Correlation coefficient` and `Linear Regression`.

STATISTICAL HYPOTHESIS TEST

  • Hypothesis: The timing of the spikes in COVID-19 cases correlates to the spread of mis/disinformation about food supplements and OTC drugs being able to cure COVID-19.
  • Predictor Variable: The spread of mis/disinformation about food supplements and OTC drugs as cure for COVID-19. The variable uses Quantitative data to find the amount of twitter posts that spread mis/disinformation.
  • Outcome Variable: Peak COVID-19 cases Quantitative data. This variable represents the occurrence and timing of increases in COVID-19 cases.

    • Pearson's Correlation Coefficient

    This tests is a statistical measure that quantifies the strength and direction of the linear relationship between two continuous variables.The magnitude of the correlation coefficient represents the strength of the relationship.

    Linear Regression

    Linear regression is a statistical modeling technique used to analyze the relationship between a dependent variable and one or more independent variables. It aims to find the best-fitting linear equation that represents the linear association between the variables. The resulting equation can be used for prediction, understanding the relationship between variables, and making inferences about the data.


    Assumptions of Statistical Testing

  • Level of Measurement: Interval and Ratio level
  • Linear Relationship
  • Normal Distribution of both variables
  • No Outliers




    • Results of the Pearson's Correlation Coefficient

    Despite using data normalization and outlier treatment, the following are the results of the statistical hypothesis testing:

  • Pearson's Correlation Coefficient = 0.027
  • p-value = 0.87
  • Conclusion: The coefficient obtained indicates a relatively small effect size or association. However, it is important to note that the p-value suggests that this coefficient is not statistically significant. This implies that the observed relationship between the variables may likely be due to chance or random variation.
  • Violations: The statistical test assumes a linear relationship between variables which have not been properly met.



    • Results of the Linear Regression

    FOR MONTHLY TWEET COUNT

  • Linear Regression: RMSE=2.23, R2=0.40
  • P(x1): 3.299192922816914e-05
  • Conclusion: The linear regression analysis reveals a significant relationship between the predictor and the response variables. The R-squared value of 0.40 indicates that 40% of the variability in the response variable can be explained by the predictor variable implying that there is a moderate impact on the frequency of mis/disinformation in relation to the date posted.

    • FOR MONTHLY COVID CASES

  • Linear Regression: RMSE=98609.93, R2=0.01
  • P(x1): 0.5163843401341183
